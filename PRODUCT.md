# Product

## Register

product

## Users

RefusalRail is for hackathon judges, RWA application builders, agentic wallet builders, and self-custody finance users who need automation with visible limits. Their context is a high-trust financial workflow: an agent may claim distributions or sweep proceeds, but it must not sell principal or route unsafe calldata during a market shock.

## Product Purpose

RefusalRail lets a user run a bounded RWA standing-action agent and prove that unsafe actions are refused by policy. Success means a first-time judge can choose a shock condition, let the agent try, see the action rejected, and inspect a durable refusal receipt within 60 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, forensic, restrained. The interface should feel like a financial flight recorder and policy workbench, not a trading terminal or AI chatbot.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic dark trading dashboards, APY card walls, purple AI gradients, chat-first assistants, abstract node diagrams, and speculative portfolio language. The product should not look like a brokerage clone, compliance PDF viewer, or wallet-connect-only demo.

## Design Principles

- The refusal is the product: make the failed trade more visible than any success state.
- Evidence before explanation: every claim should point to a receipt, hash, policy, event, or test.
- Keep the user in a bounded workbench: narrow actions, clear policy scope, explicit shock state.
- Make safety feel executable: policy checks must be shown as state transitions, not as paragraphs.
- The judge path wins first: the 60-second hero flow takes priority over secondary configuration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for all text and controls. Do not rely on red alone for refusal state; pair color with labels, icons, and receipt status. Respect reduced-motion preferences, keep keyboard focus visible, and make the hero path usable without wallet connection by supporting a guest demo identity.
